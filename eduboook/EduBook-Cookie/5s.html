  • The first impression when holding the book is that the cover is super beautiful and romantic, the outer cover is a story and the inner cover is another story, both are beautiful and have a sad feeling. "5 centimeters per second" is divided into 3 parts with 3 different stories about the life of the male protagonist Toono Takaki. The beginning is a very pure, gentle and romantic first love story that everyone seems to have experienced. Takaki and Akari have been together all the time when they were innocent children from 4th grade to 8th grade. The two people are similar in soul and personality. The love story is associated with a romantic, profound, haunting image of cherry blossoms. The second story is told through the narration of Takaki's high school classmate - Kanae. Kanae has had a crush on her classmate for a long time and has been quietly observing him. But Takaki always kept a certain distance from Kanae, not far, not near, enough for Kanae to understand that his feelings were not in her. The story evokes feelings of sadness and quiet moments, because Takaki can't forget the past, he missed the opportunity to come to his love. The last story is when Takaki has grown up and gone to work. He still can't forget his first love and always seems to want to find someone like that girl. That's why it seems that none of his relationships can last... 5cm/s is told by the author in slow narration, causing readers to slow down to ponder each word. The author's psychological description is extremely subtle and profound, which seems to make the pain and gentle emotions more deeply ingrained in the reader's heart.
